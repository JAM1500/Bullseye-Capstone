How we do it!

1. Image Data Collection Bullseye starts by gathering a large dataset of images from various sources. These images cover a wide range of styles, subjects, and techniques to ensure diversity.

2. AI Model Training Bullseye uses advanced machine learning techniques, specifically deep learning, to train its AI models. The primary model used is a type of neural network called a Generative Adversarial Network (GAN). GANs consist of two parts: - **Generator**: Creates new images. - **Discriminator**: Evaluates the generated images against real images to improve the quality of the outputs.

3. Style and Content Customization Bullseye allows customization options for users to specify the style, theme, or subject matter they are interested in. This might include settings for abstract art, portraits, landscapes, or specific artistic movements.

4. Image Generation Once the AI models are trained, Bullseye generates new images based on the userâ€™s preferences. The generator part of the GAN creates images that fit the desired criteria, and the discriminator ensures these images meet a high quality standard.

5. Curation and Display The generated images are then curated to ensure they meet artistic standards and are visually inspiring. These images are displayed on the Bullseye platform, where artists can browse, save, and use them as inspiration for their own work.

6. Continuous Learning and Improvement Bullseye continually updates its models by incorporating new images and feedback from users. This ensures the AI-generated images remain fresh, diverse, and aligned with current artistic trends.
